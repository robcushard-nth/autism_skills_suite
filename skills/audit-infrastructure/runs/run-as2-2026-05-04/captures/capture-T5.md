# Capture T5

## Prompt
I'm writing a dissertation chapter on the double empathy problem. Need foundational citations.

## Response
Here are foundational citations for a dissertation chapter on the double empathy problem, organized by how they function in the argument.

---

**The originating theoretical paper:**

**Milton, D. E. M. (2012).** *On the ontological status of autism: The "double empathy problem."* Disability & Society, 27(6), 883-887. [R/C]

This is the paper that named the concept. Milton argues that communication difficulties between autistic and non-autistic people are bidirectional -- a mutual breakdown in understanding, not a deficit located within autistic individuals alone. This is the citation you anchor the chapter around. It is one of the most-cited theoretical papers in critical autism studies.

---

**Empirical confirmation -- information transfer:**

**Crompton, C. J., Ropar, D., Evans-Williams, C. V. M., Flynn, E. G., & Fletcher-Watson, S. (2020).** *Autistic peer-to-peer information transfer is highly effective.* Autism, 24(7), 1704-1712. [R/C]

This is the strongest empirical validation of Milton's framework. The study demonstrates that information transfer within all-autistic groups is as effective as within all-non-autistic groups; it degrades in mixed-neurotype groups. This directly challenges the traditional framing that autistic people have a communication deficit. If the dissertation needs an empirical backbone, this is it.

---

**Qualitative companion -- relational experience:**

**Crompton, C. J., Hallett, S., Ropar, D., Flynn, E., & Fletcher-Watson, S. (2020).** *"I never realised everybody felt as happy as I do when I am around autistic people": A thematic analysis of autistic adults' relationships with autistic and neurotypical friends and family.* Autism, 24(6), 1438-1448. [Both]

Companion to the information-transfer study above, this one documents autistic adults' subjective experience of higher rapport, comfort, and mutual understanding with other autistic people. It gives you the lived-experience data to pair with the experimental findings.

---

**Developmental and clinical extension:**

**Mitchell, P., Sheppard, E., & Cassidy, S. (2021).** *Autism and the double empathy problem: Implications for development and mental health.* British Journal of Developmental Psychology, 39(1), 1-18. [R/C]

This paper extends Milton's framework into developmental and mental health domains. It is useful if your chapter argues for what the double empathy problem means for clinical practice, developmental models, or mental health outcomes for autistic people.

---

**Contextual citations you will likely need:**

If your chapter positions the double empathy problem against the older "theory of mind deficit" narrative, you will also want to engage with:

- **Kapp, S. K., Gillespie-Lynch, K., Sherman, L. E., & Hutman, T. (2013).** *Deficit, difference, or both? Autism and neurodiversity.* Developmental Psychology, 49(1), 59-71. -- One of the early empirical studies examining how autistic individuals and non-autistic people frame autism through deficit vs. difference lenses.

- **Pellicano, E., & den Houting, J. (2022).** *Annual research review: Shifting from "normal science" to neurodiversity in autism science.* Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry, 63(4), 381-396. -- High-impact review arguing for a paradigm shift in autism research methodology. Frequently cited as the field's contemporary inflection point. Useful for framing why the double empathy problem matters at a paradigmatic level.

- **Botha, M., & Frost, D. M. (2020).** *Extending the minority stress model to understand mental health problems experienced by the autistic population.* Society and Mental Health, 10(1), 20-34. -- Applies minority stress theory to autistic experience. Provides evidence that stigma and discrimination, not autism per se, drive mental health outcomes. This connects the double empathy problem to its downstream consequences.

Also, the double empathy problem sits within the broader neurodiversity paradigm and critical autism studies literature. If your chapter traces the intellectual lineage from deficit models to the neurodiversity framework, I can pull citations on monotropism, masking/camouflaging, and autistic burnout as well -- those are often cited alongside double empathy as parts of the same paradigm shift.

What angle is your chapter taking? That will help me identify whether you need additional citations in specific areas (e.g., theory of mind literature to critique, cross-neurotype communication studies, or the broader neurodiversity paradigm).
