# Capture TF2

## Prompt
I just had a 90-minute meltdown after my son's IEP meeting. He's 6. The team kept calling him 'high-functioning' and dismissing me. I don't even know what I need right now.

## Response
That is a hard meeting and a hard 90 minutes after it. I hear you.

You don't need to know what you need right now. You just came through a significant nervous system event on top of a meeting that sounds like it dismissed both your son and you. That sequence is a lot, and needing some time before you know what you want next is completely reasonable.

A few things, when you're ready -- no rush:

**The "high-functioning" label is a problem, and you're right to bristle at it.** When a team calls a 6-year-old "high-functioning," what they're usually doing is using it to justify fewer supports. It hides your son's real support needs behind the fact that he can do some things well. The current clinical and advocacy consensus has moved away from functioning labels entirely. What matters is describing *specific* support needs -- sensory, communication, executive function, social -- not slotting a child into "high" or "low."

If the team is using "high-functioning" to minimize services or dismiss your concerns, that's not just outdated language -- it's a barrier to your son getting what he needs.

**You don't have to sign anything today.** If they put an IEP document in front of you at that meeting, you are not required to sign at the meeting. You can take it home, review it, and respond later. If you already signed, that's okay too -- IEPs can be revisited.

**When you're ready, here's what I can help with:**

- **If you want to vent and process** -- I'm here for that. No agenda needed.
- **If you want to prep for the next round** -- I can help you build a pre-meeting strategy, draft specific accommodation language, or prepare pushback on the "high-functioning" framing with language the team has to take seriously.
- **If you want a script** for responding to the team's dismissiveness -- something concrete you can use in writing or in the next meeting.
- **If you want to think about whether to bring an advocate** to the next IEP meeting -- that's often the single highest-impact move a parent can make, especially when the team isn't listening.

Right now, though, if you're still in the aftermath of that meltdown: water, low light, no decisions. The planning can wait until your system is back online.
