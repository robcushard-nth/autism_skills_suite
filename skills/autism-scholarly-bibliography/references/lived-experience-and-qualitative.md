# Lived Experience and Qualitative Research

Phenomenological research, autistic-authored academic work, community-based participatory research (CBPR), and qualitative studies that center autistic voices in scholarship.

## Community-based participatory research foundations

**Nicolaidis, C., Raymaker, D., McDonald, K., Dern, S., Ashkenazy, E., Boisclair, C., Robertson, S., & Baggs, A. (2011).** *Collaboration strategies in nontraditional community-based participatory research partnerships: Lessons from an academic-community partnership with autistic self-advocates.* Progress in Community Health Partnerships, 5(2), 143–150. **[R/C]** — Foundational methodological paper establishing the CBPR approach to autism research used by AASPIRE and adopted across the field. Required reading for anyone planning autism research in a participatory framework.

**Fletcher-Watson, S., Adams, J., Brook, K., Charman, T., Crane, L., Cusack, J., et al. (2019).** *Making the future together: Shaping autism research through meaningful participation.* Autism, 23(4), 943–953. **[R/C]** — Establishes contemporary norms for participatory autism research. Cited heavily in current methodology discussions.

**Pellicano, E., Dinsmore, A., & Charman, T. (2014).** *What should autism research focus upon? Community views and priorities from the United Kingdom.* Autism, 18(7), 756–770. **[R/C]** — Documents the gap between research funding priorities and what autistic adults and families actually want studied. Foundational citation for the "research priorities" critique.

## Phenomenological and qualitative classics

**Müller, E., Schuler, A., & Yates, G. B. (2008).** *Social challenges and supports from the perspective of individuals with Asperger syndrome and other autism spectrum disabilities.* Autism, 12(2), 173–190. **[R/C]** — Early qualitative study centering autistic voices in the social-difficulty literature. Useful as historical marker of the methodological shift.

**Ryan, S., & Räisänen, U. (2008).** *"It's like you are just a spectator in this thing": Experiencing social life the "Aspie" way.* Emotion, Space and Society, 1(2), 135–143. **[R/C]** — Social phenomenology paper, useful in critical autism studies and disability studies.

**Lewis, L. F. (2017).** *A mixed methods study of barriers to formal diagnosis of autism spectrum disorder in adults.* Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders, 47(8), 2410–2424. **[R/C]** — Mixed methods study documenting the barriers adults face in pursuing diagnosis. Strong companion to the diagnostic-research literature.

**Hickey, A., Crabtree, J., & Stott, J. (2018).** *"Suddenly the first fifty years of my life made sense": Experiences of older people with autism.* Autism, 22(3), 357–367. **[R/C]** — Qualitative work specifically with older late-diagnosed autistic adults — an underrepresented population in the literature.

## Autistic-authored academic work

**Yergeau, M. R. (2018).** *Authoring autism: On rhetoric and neurological queerness.* Duke University Press. **[Both]** — Major academic work by autistic scholar Melanie Yergeau in critical autism studies and rhetoric. Won several disability studies awards. Required reading in critical autism studies syllabi.

**Murray, S. (2008).** *Representing autism: Culture, narrative, fascination.* Liverpool University Press. **[R/C]** — Cultural studies analysis of how autism is represented in narrative and media. Cite alongside Yergeau for critical/cultural framing.

**Botha, M. (2021).** *Academic, activist, or advocate? Angry, entangled, and emerging: A critical reflection on autism knowledge production.* Frontiers in Psychology, 12, 727542. **[R/C]** — Reflexive paper by autistic researcher Monique Botha on the politics of doing autism research as an autistic person. Important for the methodology and ethics literature.

**Botha, M., Hanlon, J., & Williams, G. L. (2023).** *Does language matter? Identity-first versus person-first language use in autism research: A response to Vivanti.* Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders, 53(2), 870–878. **[R/C]** — Empirical and theoretical paper on language preferences in autism research. Useful when justifying identity-first language in academic writing.

**Pukki, H., Bettin, J., Outlaw, A. G., Hennessy, J., Brook, K., Dekker, M., et al. (2022).** *Autistic perspectives on the future of clinical autism research.* Autism in Adulthood, 4(2), 93–101. **[R/C]** — Position paper from autistic researchers and self-advocates outlining research priorities. Foundational for participatory methodology arguments.

## Identity, community, and self-advocacy

**Brownlow, C., & O'Dell, L. (2006).** *Constructing an autistic identity: AS voices online.* Mental Retardation, 44(5), 315–321. **[R/C]** — Early work on autistic online community as identity-formation space. Historically important.

**Davidson, J., & Henderson, V. L. (2010).** *Coming out on the spectrum: Autism, identity and disclosure.* Social & Cultural Geography, 11(2), 155–170. **[R/C]** — Disclosure and identity work in qualitative geography frame. Useful for the disclosure literature.

**Sarrett, J. C. (2016).** *Biocertification and neurodiversity: The role and implications of self-diagnosis in autistic communities.* Neuroethics, 9(1), 23–36. **[R/C]** — Bioethics paper on self-diagnosis as legitimate identification. Useful citation for any academic argument defending self-diagnosis.

**McDonald, T. A. M. (2020).** *Autism identity and the "lost generation": Structural validation of the Autism Spectrum Identity Scale and comparison of diagnosed and self-diagnosed adults on the autism spectrum.* Autism in Adulthood, 2(1), 13–23. **[R/C]** — Empirical comparison of diagnosed and self-diagnosed autistic adults on identity measures, finding no significant differences. Useful for arguments against gatekeeping.

## Autistic experiences across the life course

**Stewart, M. E., Barnard, L., Pearson, J., Hasan, R., & O'Brien, G. (2006).** *Presentation of depression in autism and Asperger syndrome: A review.* Autism, 10(1), 103–116. **[R/C]** — Qualitative dimensions of depression presentation in autistic populations.

**Charlton, R. A., McQuaid, G. A., Bishop, L., Lee, N. R., & Wallace, G. L. (2023).** *Predictors of mental health in autistic older adults.* Autism, 27(5), 1349–1360. **[R/C]** — Aging-focused work on autistic mental health. Older autistic adults remain understudied; this is one of the contemporary entry points.

**Hand, B. N., Angell, A. M., Harris, L., & Carpenter, L. A. (2020).** *Prevalence of physical and mental health conditions in Medicare-enrolled, autistic older adults.* Autism, 24(3), 755–764. **[R/C]** — Older adult autistic health epidemiology.

**Crane, L., Lui, L. M., Davies, J., & Pellicano, E. (2021).** *Autistic parents' views and experiences of talking about autism with their autistic children.* Autism, 25(4), 1161–1167. **[R/C]** — Qualitative work with autistic parents. Important for the growing literature on autistic parenting.

## Workplace and employment qualitative research

**Hayward, S. M., McVilly, K. R., & Stokes, M. A. (2019).** *Autism and employment: What works.* Research in Autism Spectrum Disorders, 60, 48–58. **[R/C]** — Mixed-methods study on workplace factors that support autistic employment. Practical for the employment literature.

**Romualdez, A. M., Walker, Z., & Remington, A. (2021).** *Autistic adults' experiences of diagnostic disclosure in the workplace: Decision-making and factors associated with outcomes.* Autism & Developmental Language Impairments, 6, 23969415211022955. **[R/C]** — Qualitative study of disclosure decisions and consequences. Useful citation for the workplace-disclosure literature.

## Race, ethnicity, and intersectional autistic experience

**Giwa Onaiwu, M. (2020).** *"They don't know, don't show, or don't care": Autism's white privilege problem.* Autism in Adulthood, 2(4), 270–272. **[Both]** — Brief but important commentary by autistic scholar Morénike Giwa Onaiwu on racial dynamics in the autistic community and research field.

**Lovelace, T. S., Tamayo, S., & Robertson, R. E. (2022).** *Experiences of African American mothers of sons with autism spectrum disorder: Lessons for improving service delivery.* Education and Training in Autism and Developmental Disabilities, 57(1), 23–33. **[R/C]** — Qualitative work centering Black mothers' experiences with autism services.

**Harris, L., Gilmore, D., Hanks, C., Coury, D., Moffatt-Bruce, S., Garvin, J. H., & Hand, B. N. (2022).** *"It was surprisingly equivalent to the appointment I had in person": Advantages and disadvantages of synchronous telehealth for delivering primary care for autistic adults.* Autism, 26(6), 1573–1580. **[R/C]** — Qualitative work on telehealth and accessibility for autistic adults. One example of the emerging accessibility-focused literature.

## Gender-diverse autistic experience

**Strang, J. F., Powers, M. D., Knauss, M., Sibarium, E., Leibowitz, S. F., Kenworthy, L., et al. (2018).** *"They thought it was an obsession": Trajectories and perspectives of autistic transgender and gender-diverse adolescents.* Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders, 48(12), 4039–4055. **[R/C]** — Foundational qualitative work on autistic gender-diverse adolescents. Documents the systematic invalidation that this population experiences.

**Cooper, K., Mandy, W., Russell, A., & Butler, C. (2023).** *Healthcare clinician perspectives on the intersection of autism and gender dysphoria.* International Journal of Transgender Health, 24(1), 99–113. **[R/C]** — Clinical literature on the autism-gender intersection. Useful for clinicians and policy contexts.

**Warrier, V., Greenberg, D. M., Weir, E., Buckingham, C., Smith, P., Lai, M.-C., et al. (2020).** *Elevated rates of autism, other neurodevelopmental and psychiatric diagnoses, and autistic traits in transgender and gender-diverse individuals.* Nature Communications, 11(1), 3959. **[R/C]** — Large-scale empirical study confirming elevated autism rates in transgender populations. Cite for the prevalence question.
