# Contributing to the Autism Skills Suite

Thank you for considering contributing. This project depends on review and refinement from people whose expertise the maintainer does not have — autistic adults living the experience, neurodiversity-affirming clinicians, educators, and researchers.

This document explains how to contribute and how decisions get made.

---

## Governance

This is a **maintainer-led** project, not a consensus-driven one.

- **Maintainer:** Rob Cushard, NTH AI Dynamics
- **Final decisions** on content changes rest with the maintainer
- **Why this model:** The skills must hold a coherent affirming framework, consistent voice, and intact safety guardrails. Open consensus on contested questions (ABA framing, language conventions, recommended sources) tends to drift toward compromise that weakens the affirming stance.

The maintainer commits to:

- Reading every issue and pull request
- Responding within a reasonable window (typically two weeks)
- Explaining the reasoning when declining a contribution
- Crediting accepted contributions in the changelog
- Holding the framework consistent rather than chasing every new request

The maintainer does not commit to:

- Accepting every contribution
- Debating positions that conflict with the affirming framework or the safety guardrails (functioning labels, ABA-as-default, cure framing)
- Adding content that weakens crisis routing or pseudoscience refusal

## What we welcome

### From autistic adults

The most valuable contributions. If you read these skills and the framing misses, the language is wrong, the scripts don't sound like real autistic experience, or the recommendations don't match your life — please tell us.

Open a **Content Feedback** issue and describe:

- What skill and section you're flagging
- What's wrong (specific quote helps)
- What would land better
- Your perspective (you can stay anonymous; we don't need credentials)

### From clinicians, therapists, OTs, SLPs

Clinical accuracy corrections, citation updates, recommendations to add or remove. Open a **Content Feedback** issue with:

- The specific claim or recommendation in question
- The corrected information with citation
- Your professional context (helps us calibrate confidence)

### From educators and special education professionals

The IEP/504 toolkit and school-advocacy references benefit most from current practitioner experience. Same process — Content Feedback issue.

### From researchers

The scholarly bibliography needs ongoing maintenance. New citations, retracted ones, methodology critiques, currency updates. Open a **Bibliography Update** issue with full citation, audience tag, and a one-line framing.

### From translators

Localized versions are welcome. Open an issue first to coordinate (we want to avoid duplicate translation efforts and want to ensure quality review).

### Code, infrastructure, and tooling

Pull requests welcome for:

- Repo structure and CI improvements
- Better installation scripts for different environments
- Validation tools for skill files
- Documentation improvements

## How to contribute

### Small content changes (typo, clarification, link fix)

1. Open a pull request directly with the change
2. Brief description of what and why
3. The maintainer will review, often merge same-week

### Substantive content changes (framing, recommendations, new sections)

1. **Open an issue first** describing what you want to change and why
2. Wait for maintainer response (typically within two weeks)
3. If the change is welcome, work on it as a pull request
4. If the change isn't a fit, the maintainer will explain why

This protects your time — substantive PRs are easier to write after we've agreed they're going somewhere.

### Issues only (no code)

Sometimes you have feedback but not the time or skill to write the change yourself. That's fine. A well-written issue describing a problem is genuinely valuable. The maintainer or another contributor may pick it up.

## What's out of scope

The following won't be accepted, regardless of how well-argued:

- **Removal of the affirming framework** — neurodiversity is the foundation, not negotiable
- **Re-introduction of functioning labels** as primary descriptors
- **ABA endorsement** as default recommendation; the controversy framing stays
- **Cure or recovery framing**
- **Pseudoscience legitimization** (MMS, chelation, hyperbaric for autism, etc.)
- **Removal or weakening of crisis routing** in any skill
- **Removal of safety guardrails** broadly
- **Vaccine causation** content presented as legitimate science

If your contribution depends on one of these, it's not the right project for it. There are other autism resources with different framings.

## Code of Conduct

See [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md). Bottom line: respectful, direct communication. Direct disagreement is welcome; personal attacks are not.

This is a topic where strong opinions exist on multiple sides. We expect contributors to engage with positions they disagree with — not by demanding the project change to match their view, but by explaining their reasoning and accepting that the maintainer holds final say.

---

## Recognition

Contributors are credited:

- In `CHANGELOG.md` for accepted contributions
- In a `CONTRIBUTORS.md` file (created when contributions reach a threshold)
- In commit attribution

Anonymous contributions are accepted. If you don't want your name attached, say so in the issue or PR — we'll credit as "Anonymous Contributor."
