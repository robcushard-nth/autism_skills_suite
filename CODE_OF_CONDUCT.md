# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

This project welcomes contributors with a wide range of perspectives, professional backgrounds, and lived experiences. We commit to making participation harassment-free for everyone, regardless of:

- Autism status, neurotype, or disability
- Age, gender identity, sexual orientation
- Race, ethnicity, nationality
- Religion or lack thereof
- Professional credentials or lack thereof
- Communication style

## Direct communication is welcome

Many autistic contributors find indirect, heavily softened communication harder to engage with than direct, specific feedback. This project supports direct communication — including direct disagreement — as a feature, not a problem.

What this means:

- "I think this section is wrong because X" is fine
- "I disagree with this framing" is fine
- "This recommendation could harm someone" is exactly the kind of feedback we want
- Specific, factual critique is welcome

## What's not acceptable

- Personal attacks, harassment, or abuse
- Demands to change the affirming framework or safety guardrails (these are foundational; see CONTRIBUTING.md)
- Discrimination or slurs targeting any group
- Doxxing, threats, or intimidation
- Sustained derailment of issues or PRs
- Treating autistic contributors as less credible than non-autistic contributors
- Treating self-diagnosed autistic contributors as less credible than formally diagnosed contributors
- Insistence that autistic adults' lived experience is less valuable than clinical expertise
- Insistence that clinical expertise is less valuable than autistic adults' lived experience

The last two cut both directions. Both forms of knowledge are valued.

## Specific to this topic

This project sits in contested intellectual territory. We expect:

- Disagreement about ABA, masking, identity language, and intervention approaches will arise
- Some contributors will hold positions the project does not adopt
- The maintainer will sometimes decline contributions

When this happens, the standard is: explain your position once, accept the decision, move on. Repeated re-litigation of decided questions is not a productive use of the issue tracker.

## Reporting

If someone violates this code of conduct, contact the maintainer directly: rob.cushard@nthsimplesolution.com

Reports will be:
- Acknowledged within 72 hours
- Handled confidentially
- Investigated proportionally to the severity
- Addressed with action (warning, temporary or permanent ban from the repo) where warranted

## Enforcement

The maintainer may take any action deemed appropriate to maintain a productive environment, up to and including blocking contributors from the repository.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the Contributor Covenant 2.1, modified to reflect the specific context of this project — particularly the affirming framework, the recognition of multiple legitimate forms of expertise, and the support for direct communication styles.
